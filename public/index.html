01 — The Mission

Why We're Building This

The Problem

Nibbles makes the safest baby feeding products in India — LFGB + FDA + NABL certified silicone. But Indian parents searching "best baby bottle India" find plastic brands with bigger ad budgets, not the safest option. Organic content is the equalizer.

"When you can't outspend, you out-content. Every optimized blog is a free sales rep working 24/7."

The Solution: Automated Organic Growth Engine

Instead of manually writing blogs, we built a fully automated marketing engine that:

  • Updates 3 existing articles daily — turning thin content into ranking machines
  • Creates 1 new article daily — expanding keyword coverage every single day
  • Uses E-E-A-T methodology — Google's own quality framework, baked into every article
  • Targets Indian parents specifically — local context, INR pricing, Indian certifications
  • Runs on autopilot — Claude Scheduled Task fires at 7:30 AM IST, zero manual work

02 — How It Works

The Daily Execution Flow

01

Fetch All Articles

GraphQL pulls every article from Shopify. We analyze titles, tags, body content, and structure.

02

Identify Weakest

Algorithm finds the 3 oldest articles missing: Key Takeaways, FAQ, product links, proper tags, comparison tables.

03

Rewrite with E-E-A-T

Full rewrite: SEO title, Key Takeaways box, expert content, product images, comparison tables, FAQ section.

04

Create New Post

1 new article targeting high-volume search queries. Topic rotates daily: feeding guides, comparisons, seasonal, gift guides.

05

Publish & Index

Push to Shopify via GraphQL mutations. Redirect old URLs. Update publish date for freshness signal.

06

Report & Queue

Summary of changes + next 3 articles queued for tomorrow. The cycle never stops.

03 — Tag Strategy

Smart Tags for Maximum Reach

Why Not Just "India 2026" on Everything?

Generic tags like "India 2026" on every article is a wasted opportunity. Each article should have unique, intent-driven tags that match what real parents search for. Here's our actual strategy:

  • Primary keyword tag — The main search term ("anti colic bottle for newborn")
  • Problem/solution tags — What parents are worried about ("baby gas relief", "colic remedies")
  • Age-based tags — Target specific stages ("0-3 month feeding", "6 month weaning")
  • Product category tags — Connect to collection pages ("silicone bottle", "baby teether")
  • Long-tail question tags — Voice search targets ("how to sterilize baby bottles", "best bottle for breastfed baby")
  • Certification tags — Trust signals ("BPA free", "LFGB certified", "food grade silicone")
  • 1-2 location tags — Just enough for local SEO ("India", "Indian parents")
  • Seasonal tags when relevant — Timely content ("monsoon baby care", "summer feeding tips")
10-12 unique, search-intent tags per article > 10 generic tags repeated everywhere
04 — E-E-A-T Explained

Google's Quality Framework

Experience

Content reflects real Indian parenting — monsoon sterilisation challenges, joint family dynamics, boiling as primary sterilisation method, local city references.

Expertise

Paediatrician-backed advice, developmental milestones, feeding schedules by age, certification details (LFGB, FDA, NABL, BIS).

Authoritativeness

Data-driven content — comparison tables, statistics, structured FAQ sections, product specifications, internal cross-linking.

Trustworthiness

Honest product comparisons (silicone vs plastic vs steel), full certification transparency, no exaggerated health claims.

05 — Article Anatomy

Every Article Must Have

Mandatory Structure (Non-Negotiable)

  • H1 Title — Primary keyword + contextual location + current year
  • Key Takeaways Box — Styled div, 4-5 bullet points, immediately after H1
  • Introduction — Primary keyword in first 100 words
  • H2 > H3 Sections — Proper hierarchy, never skip levels
  • Product Images + Links — 3+ internal product links with title attributes
  • Comparison Tables — At least 1 for rich snippet eligibility
  • FAQ Section — 5-6 questions, 40-60 word answers, targets voice search
  • Author: Team Nibbles — Always
  • Publish Date: Today — Freshness signal for Google
  • 10-12 Unique Tags — Intent-driven, not generic
